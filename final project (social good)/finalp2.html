ARTICLE SECTION: Making Ends Meet

There are so many people in San Francisco that are stuck in low paying jobs and can't make ends meet. 171.5 hours of minimum wage job needed to rent market rate in San Francisco This is impossible because there is only 168 hours in a week. What is really suprising is this. In San Francisco, households earning $117,000 qualify as low income This means a couple would need to make $29 an hour each to make ends meet which is almost double that of $15 minimum wage.

Footer Section: Summary

We need more youth employment services to help give youth work experience at a young age. People need to volunteer at nonprofits to help youth get in demand hard skills to get good jobs. There needs to be more funding for youth employment and free college. According to Yearup, there are 5 million young adults disconnected from jobs when there are 12 million jobs which is an opportunity divide.
